## TL;DR

Obsidian works well in enterprise environments — but cloud sync features are typically restricted for vaults containing company data.

## Details

**Availability:**
- Obsidian is free for personal use; commercial licenses are available for enterprise
- Many large companies have approved Obsidian through their security review process
- Licenses are typically available through internal IT self-service portals

**Key constraint:** Obsidian Sync (their paid cloud service) is usually not approved for vaults containing company-confidential data. This would upload sensitive content to a third-party cloud. Personal vaults with no work data can use Sync freely.

**Enterprise plugins:**
- Some companies build internal plugins for employee directories, meeting integrations, and internal auth
- The community plugin ecosystem is large — check your company's policy on which are allowed

**Best practices:**
- Keep work vaults local (no third-party cloud sync)
- Check your company's data handling policies before installing community plugins
- Consider Obsidian as a local-first alternative to cloud-based note tools

## When This Matters

When evaluating note-taking tools for work use, or when determining what Obsidian features are safe to use with company data.
